Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB)

At the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB), I am advancing the understanding of nucleosynthesis in Type I X-ray bursts, focusing on critical reaction rates such as 15O(α, γ)19Ne, which governs breakout from the hot CNO cycle and the onset of the rp-process. I led FRIB experiment E21072, which utilized the Gaseous Detector with GErmanium Tagging (GADGET II), a cutting-edge detection system that I designed, built, and commissioned to measure the β-delayed (β p α) decay of 20Mg. By capturing the detailed 3D event topology, we determined a finite α-particle branching ratio, demonstrating both experimental innovation and my leadership in executing this complex measurement.

In addition, at FRIB my group will be conducting experiment E25046 using β-delayed proton spectroscopy of 58Zn to identify resonances in 58Cu, directly constraining the 57Ni(p, γ)58Cu reaction rate. This reaction plays a vital role in the synthesis of 44Ti in core-collapse supernovae, influencing energy generation and neutron star crust cooling.
